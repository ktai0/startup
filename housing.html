アメリカで家を探す方法

1) Prepare before you search

  • Decide budget, move-in date, neighborhoods, and “must-haves” (parking, pets, utilities included, furnished, etc.).
  • Use a folder (or save in Notes) with message templates and screenshot / copy of each listing you like.

2) Search smart on Facebook Marketplace

  • Use the Housing category and filters (location radius, price, home type). Many property managers and landlords post there as well
  • Try multiple keyword combinations (city + “for rent”, “available now”, “furnished”, “utilities included”, etc.). Save searches and enable alerts for new listings.

3) Quick Vet

  • Check listing date (newer is better), price (too low = scam), description (vague = red flag), and photos (few or none = red flag).
  • Google the address to see if it’s real, check Street View, and look for reviews of the property or landlord.
  • Download or copy a listing photo and run a reverse image search (Google Images or TinEye) to see if the photo appears elsewhere (often a sign the images are stolen).
  • Read the description. Pay attention to hidden fees (e.g. utilities, parking passes, etc)
  • Ask questions via Messenger: Is it still available? When can I see it? How many people are applying? What’s the application process?

    • often you will be buying a contract from someone who has already purchased a prior contract. So you need ot talk extensively with the person on chat.

Red flags (stop immediately if you see these)

  • Price is way below market rate
  • Landlord/manager can’t show you the unit in person or via video
  • Landlord/manager wants you to pay via wire transfer, gift card, or cash
  • Listing has no photos or only stock photos
  • Landlord/manager is out of the country or can’t meet you in person
  • Poster’s Facebook profile is brand new, minimal activity, or uses stolen photos.
  • requests deposit before signing contract
